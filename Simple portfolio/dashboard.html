Recent Projects

Project 1

The Fatrat Attack

The FatRat Attack, also known as "The FatRat Exploitation Tool," is a powerful and versatile open-source hacking tool used by cybersecurity professionals and ethical hackers for penetration testing and system exploitation. Developed in Python, The FatRat offers a wide range of attack vectors, including payload generation, remote access, and post-exploitation functionalities. The tool allows users to create custom payloads and inject them into targeted systems to exploit vulnerabilities, gain unauthorized access, and execute various commands. It supports a variety of operating systems, making it effective across different platforms.

Project 2

Denial Of Service

A Denial of Service (DoS) attack is a malicious attempt to disrupt the normal functioning of a targeted computer system, network, or service, rendering it temporarily or permanently inaccessible to legitimate users. The primary objective of a DoS attack is to overwhelm the target's resources, causing it to become unresponsive or crash.

Project 3

Network Security

Network Security, refers to the measures and practices implemented to protect a computer network from unauthorized access, misuse, modification, or disruption. It involves using various technologies, policies, and procedures to ensure the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data and resources within the network.

Project 4

Network Analysis

Network traffic analysis or packet analysis, is the process of capturing, inspecting, and interpreting data packets that flow through a computer network. It involves examining the network traffic to gain insights into the network's performance, troubleshoot issues, identify security threats, and optimize network resources.
